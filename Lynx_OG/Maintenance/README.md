# Lynx OG - Maintenance Guide

## ⚠️ Safety Warning

**Before any maintenance work:**
1. **Power off** the wheel completely.
2. **Disconnect the battery** if working near electronics.
3. The Lynx operates at **151.2V DC** — lethal voltage. Use insulated tools and proper PPE.
4. Allow capacitors to discharge before touching the controller board.

## 🔧 Routine Maintenance

### Tire Pressure
* **Recommended:** Check tire pressure before every ride.
* **Typical range:** 30-40 PSI depending on rider weight and terrain.
  - Lower pressure (~30 PSI): better grip and comfort off-road.
  - Higher pressure (~40 PSI): better efficiency and range on pavement.
* Tire is **tubeless** — check sealant level every 3-6 months and replenish as needed.

### Suspension
* **Spring Weights:** 62 lbs (Soft, <190 lbs rider) / 66 lbs (Medium, 190-220 lbs) / 70 lbs (Hard, >220 lbs).
* **Adjustment:** Rebound, compression, and preload are adjustable on the FastAce shocks (80/90mm travel).
* **Inspection:** Check shock mounting bolts periodically for tightness. Listen for unusual clunking sounds.
* **Service interval:** Shock oil should be refreshed annually or every ~3,000 miles under heavy use.

### Pedals
* Check pedal pivot bolts for tightness.
* Inspect grip studs/surface for wear.
* Lubricate pivot points if pedals become stiff to fold.

### Charging
* **Connectors:** Dual GX20/5-pin charging ports (max 16A total combined).
* **Charger:** Stock 5A charger included. Compatible with higher-amperage aftermarket chargers up to the 16A limit.
* **Battery care:** Avoid storing at 100% for extended periods. 60-80% is ideal for long-term storage.
* **Charging temperature:** Only charge between 0-45°C (32-113°F).

## 🛠️ Common Repairs

### Tire Change
> ⚠️ Tire changes on suspension EUCs require partial disassembly. Refer to the disassembly guide.

1. Remove side panels.
2. Disconnect motor phase wires and hall sensor cable (if present).
3. Remove axle bolts and extract the motor/wheel assembly.
4. Break the tubeless tire bead, swap tire, and reseat bead.
5. Add tubeless sealant before inflating.
6. Reassemble in reverse order. Torque axle bolts to spec.

*Detailed step-by-step with photos: contributions needed.*

### Pedal Replacement
* Pedals are attached to brackets on the wheel body.
* Remove bracket bolts to access pedal assemblies.
* Replacement pedals are available from eWheels and other dealers.

### Shell / Side Panel Replacement
* Side panels are held with screws and clips.
* Replacement shells available from Leaperkim or dealers.
* Consider applying conformal coating to electronics if the shell was breached by water.
